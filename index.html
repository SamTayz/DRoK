Divine Right of Kings

The Divine Right of Kings was more than just an excuse to wear a crown — it was a belief that kings were chosen by God to rule, and that questioning them was basically a sin. This idea shaped centuries of politics, power struggles, and rebellions. On this site, we break down what the Divine Right really meant, where it came from, why people believed it, and how it impacted history. You might’ve heard it mentioned in connection to Shakespeare’s Macbeth — and that’s no coincidence. When rulers start acting like they’re above everyone else (including God), things don’t always end well…
